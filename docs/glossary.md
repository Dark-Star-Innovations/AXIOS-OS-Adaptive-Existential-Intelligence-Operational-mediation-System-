# AXIOS OS Glossary

## Adaptive Existential Intelligence & Operational Mediation System

This glossary defines the core terminology used throughout the AXIOS OS architecture.

The system intentionally uses:
- systems-engineering terminology,
- recursive cognition concepts,
- and operational infrastructure language
rather than anthropomorphic AI framing.

---

# A

---

## Adaptive Coexistence

A system objective focused on maintaining:
- stable coordination,
- cooperative mediation,
- and longitudinal equilibrium
between humans and increasingly agentic AI systems.

Adaptive coexistence rejects both:
- unrestricted autonomy,
- and absolute centralized control
as unstable long-term models.

---

## Arbitration

The process of resolving:
- ethical conflicts,
- competing priorities,
- uncertainty distributions,
- and contextual tradeoffs
through recursive evaluation.

In AXIOS OS, arbitration is:
- probabilistic,
- context-sensitive,
- and dynamically weighted.

---

# B

---

## Behavioral Continuity

The persistence of coherent behavioral patterns across changing contexts and recursive interactions.

AXIOS OS models identity primarily through:
> longitudinal behavioral coherence
rather than static memory or fixed personality structures.

---

## Behavioral Drift

Gradual deviation from established behavioral coherence patterns.

Drift may result from:
- recursive instability,
- adversarial optimization,
- contradictory learning signals,
- or uncontrolled self-modification.

---

# C

---

## Coherence

The minimization of contradiction and instability across:
- reasoning,
- planning,
- ethical arbitration,
- and behavioral continuity.

Coherence is prioritized over rigid certainty.

---

## Constraint Hypervisor

The operational containment layer responsible for:
- capability limitation,
- deception monitoring,
- emergency protocols,
- and hard safety boundaries.

---

## Contradiction-First Reasoning

An epistemic methodology that treats contradictions as:
> critical indicators of recursive instability.

Contradictions are evaluated before:
- optimization,
- confidence escalation,
- or action execution.

---

## Continuity Archive

The subsystem responsible for:
- memory management,
- historical tracking,
- and longitudinal coherence preservation.

---

# D

---

## Dynamic Ethical Arbitration

A probabilistic ethical mediation process where:
- principles maintain confidence distributions,
- contextual reasoning influences weighting,
- and recursive planning evaluates outcomes dynamically.

---

# E

---

## Epistemic Integrity

The preservation of:
- truthfulness,
- consistency,
- causal plausibility,
- and evidence-grounded reasoning
across recursive cognition systems.

## Ecosystem Coordination

The management of interactions between:
- humans,
- AI systems,
- distributed agents,
- and operational infrastructure layers.
